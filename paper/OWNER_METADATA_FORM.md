# Owner Metadata Form

Fill only the fields you are comfortable making public. This form is for release preparation; it does not publish anything by itself.

## Identity and authorship

**Public author name:**  
Why required: appears on the manuscript and any preprint metadata.  
Where it appears: manuscript title page, preprint platform metadata, citation metadata.  
Optional: no, if the work is publicly released under a named author.  
Privacy implication: public identity disclosure.

**Research pseudonym, if used:**  
Why required: lets the manuscript and repository use a consistent public identity without exposing a legal name.  
Where it appears: manuscript title page, repository metadata, outreach if used.  
Optional: yes.  
Privacy implication: reduces direct identity exposure if preferred.

**ORCID:**  
Why required: useful for scholarly attribution and disambiguation.  
Where it appears: preprint metadata, manuscript title page, citation metadata.  
Optional: yes.  
Privacy implication: links the work to an existing public researcher profile.

**Affiliation:**  
Why required: expected by many preprint platforms and venues.  
Where it appears: manuscript title page, preprint metadata.  
Optional: effectively optional for informal external review, usually required for formal submission.  
Privacy implication: may reveal employer or institution.

**Contact email:**  
Why required: corresponding-author contact and review follow-up.  
Where it appears: manuscript title page, preprint metadata, possible outreach.  
Optional: not for public submission.  
Privacy implication: direct public contact surface.

**GitHub owner/handle:**  
Why required: only if the public repository will reference a maintainer handle.  
Where it appears: repository homepage, citation metadata, optional review packets.  
Optional: yes.  
Privacy implication: links the manuscript to a public coding identity.

**Corresponding author:**  
Why required: submission workflows typically require one contact point.  
Where it appears: manuscript metadata, submission systems.  
Optional: no for formal submission; yes for internal review packets.  
Privacy implication: designates a public contact person.

## Disclosure fields

**Funding statement:**  
Why required: many venues request funding disclosure.  
Where it appears: acknowledgements or dedicated funding section.  
Optional: yes if there is no funding.  
Privacy implication: may reveal funding source.

**Conflict-of-interest statement:**  
Why required: standard scholarly disclosure.  
Where it appears: manuscript disclosures or submission form.  
Optional: often mandatory even if “none.”  
Privacy implication: may reveal personal or institutional ties.

**Acknowledgements:**  
Why required: credits non-author contributors or support.  
Where it appears: manuscript acknowledgements.  
Optional: yes.  
Privacy implication: may reveal collaborators or institutions.

## Licensing and release

**License for source code:**  
Why required: public repository release is blocked without an explicit code license.  
Where it appears: top-level `LICENSE`, public release audit, README.  
Optional: no for public repository push.  
Privacy implication: none directly, but determines reuse rights.

**License for manuscript/figures/tables:**  
Why required: preprint and public-asset reuse terms should be explicit.  
Where it appears: manuscript metadata, repository docs, preprint platform if supported.  
Optional: strongly recommended.  
Privacy implication: none directly.

## Publication path

**Preprint platform:**  
Why required: determines release workflow and metadata requirements.  
Where it appears: owner release checklist and eventual preprint submission.  
Optional: yes until actual upload.  
Privacy implication: platform-specific profile exposure may apply.

**Target venue:**  
Why required: affects formatting and some disclosure fields.  
Where it appears: internal planning only unless later disclosed.  
Optional: yes.  
Privacy implication: none directly.

**Public use of “Project Alexandria”:**  
Why required: codename control.  
Where it appears: README, manuscript acknowledgements, or nowhere.  
Optional: yes.  
Privacy implication: none, but affects public branding.

**Public repository URL:**  
Why required: needed once the work is publicly hosted.  
Where it appears: manuscript, preprint, outreach templates, review packets.  
Optional: yes until public push.  
Privacy implication: exposes the repository and any linked public account.
