# Packet F: General Technical Reader Review

## Reviewer profile

This packet is for a technically strong reader who may not be a deep VSA specialist but can judge coherence, readability, structure, and claim proportionality.

## What to look for

- Is the paper understandable after one pass?
- Does it read like one coherent work rather than a pile of repo notes?
- Is the main claim clear and bounded?
- Are the conclusions proportionate and non-grandiose?

## Specific questions

1. What is the paper's main claim after one reading?
2. Where does the narrative become difficult to follow?
3. Which section feels unnecessary or under-integrated?
4. Does the conclusion actually follow from the evidence presented?
5. If you had to cut one page for clarity, where would you cut?

## Compact framing

The paper is trying to do three things at once: summarize a reproducible evidence atlas, map related literature without fake comparability, and propose a resource-accounting framework. This packet asks whether that integration works for a smart outsider.
