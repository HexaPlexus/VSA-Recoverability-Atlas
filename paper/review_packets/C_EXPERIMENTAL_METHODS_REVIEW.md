# Packet C: Experimental Methodology Review

## Reviewer profile

This packet is for readers focused on protocol integrity, seed discipline, controls, held-out boundaries, statistical proportionality, and negative-result handling.

## Sections to review

- Section 3: mapping protocol
- Section 5: repository evidence base and status discipline
- Section 11: verifier-centered architecture and portfolio semantics
- Section 15: threats to validity
- Section 16: reproducibility

## What the manuscript currently claims

- Development-only evidence is not promoted to confirmatory evidence.
- Frozen protocols, typed gates, and seed discipline are treated as scientific infrastructure.
- Several negative results are scientifically meaningful precisely because the controls were strong.
- The clean F=3 portfolio stop decision is due to measured absence of deployable complementarity, not lack of engineering effort.

## Specific questions

1. Are the experiments and reports internally valid and proportionate to their trial counts?
2. Are development-only results overstated anywhere in the main text?
3. Are the controls around equal-bit, shuffled-score, and equal-information comparisons sufficiently visible?
4. Are important protocol-repair stories handled transparently rather than buried?
5. Is any claim ledger or evidence-registry dependency underexplained for an external methods reviewer?

## Compact extract

The paper's methodology stance is that some of the most valuable outputs are stop decisions, negative results, and blocked promotions. That stance is only defensible if the controls, split discipline, and claim scopes are explicit and auditable.
