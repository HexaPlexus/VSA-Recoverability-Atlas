# Technical One-Pager

## Research question

Which additional resources actually paid for recoverability in the repository's VSA/HDC lines, and when did added architectural complexity fail to create a new nondominated operating point after cost, verification, and silent-error accounting?

## Three contributions

1. A systematic mapping / scoping review with frozen search scope, comparability classes, and transfer limits rather than a fake global leaderboard.
2. A normalized repository evidence atlas covering positive baselines, bounded negative results, exact-structure alternatives, and architectural stop decisions.
3. A recoverability-budget framing that treats dimension, precision, structure, exact side information, compute, hardware state, and abstention as explicit currencies.

## Main evidence base

- `24` normalized repository hypotheses / lines
- `39` screened literature records
- `32` included literature sources
- machine-readable evidence registry, claim ledger, failure atlas, and generated tables
- no new experiments in this stage

## Six key results

1. Dense MAP resonators are useful bounded baselines but capacity-limited in the tested F=3 envelopes.
2. Within the common clean F=3 envelope, native BCF covered the same hard instances that defeated MAP.
3. Several decoder-repair lines preserved local signal but failed cost-aware promotion against equal-bit or shuffled controls.
4. Exact first-order manifests enable safe recursive replay after retrieval, but inline packaging did not beat an ordinary sidecar DAG.
5. Verification and typed abstention are architectural seams, not cosmetic post-processing.
6. In the tested clean F=3 portfolio audit, instance-level method-selection value was not supported, and MAP-to-BCF early-exit escalation was not cost-effective on non-easy cells.

## Central limitations

- bounded development and frozen-envelope evidence, not universal theory
- heterogeneous task contracts across literature
- no physical hardware measurements in-repo
- no new confirmatory or held-out execution in this stage
