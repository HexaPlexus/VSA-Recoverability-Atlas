# Packet D: Systematic Mapping Review

## Reviewer profile

This packet is for readers familiar with systematic mapping, scoping-review methodology, evidence extraction, or comparability framing.

## Sections to review

- Section 3: review protocol and search closure
- Section 13: literature-only hardware synthesis
- Section 15: validity threats related to search and publication bias
- Section 16: reproducibility and documentation

## What the manuscript currently claims

- The literature component is a systematic mapping / scoping review, not a quantitative meta-analysis.
- A frozen search scope, typed screening rules, and comparability classes are more defensible here than a forced leaderboard.
- Literature points are used to map resource-bearing mechanisms and transfer limits, not to fabricate common-harness reproduction status.

## Specific questions

1. Is "systematic mapping" an appropriate and defensible label for the actual process used here?
2. Are the search families, inclusion criteria, and exclusion reasons sufficiently reproducible?
3. Are the comparability classes useful, or do they need simplification?
4. Which obvious literature family is still missing?
5. Does the manuscript overstate what the literature map can support?

## Compact extract

The review component is deliberately scoped and typed because the field mixes different algebras, tasks, noise contracts, and hardware assumptions. The manuscript explicitly argues that these differences block a lawful one-line global numeric ranking.
