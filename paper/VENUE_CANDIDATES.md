# Venue Candidates

This document is for discovery only. It is not a submission recommendation, and it does not estimate acceptance probability.

## Candidate categories

### 1. VSA / HDC / neural computation venues

Examples:

- [Neural Computation](https://direct.mit.edu/neco)
- [Cognitive Computation](https://link.springer.com/journal/12559)

Why they fit:

- direct relevance to VSA/HDC, compositional representation, and factorization
- appropriate audience for MAP, resonators, structured substrates, and exact-side-information contrasts

Main risk:

- the manuscript is broader than a single decoder or substrate paper
- systematic-mapping and repository-atlas elements may feel unusual unless tightened carefully

### 2. Neuromorphic / hardware-aware venues

Examples:

- [Neuromorphic Computing and Engineering](https://iopscience.iop.org/journal/2634-4386)
- hardware-facing special issues where HDC deployment is a legitimate focus

Why they fit:

- the manuscript has a bounded hardware-literature section and explicit claims about how hardware changes the frontier

Main risk:

- the repository contains no physical hardware measurements
- hardware discussion is literature synthesis only, so a hardware-first venue could perceive the empirical center of gravity as misaligned

### 3. Broad AI methodology / ML systems / empirical-methodology venues

Examples:

- [PeerJ Computer Science](https://peerj.com/computer-science/)
- journals or workshops that accept reproducibility-heavy empirical synthesis

Why they fit:

- manuscript emphasis on evidence discipline, bounded claims, failure modes, and reproducibility infrastructure

Main risk:

- some venues may expect a more application-centered workload
- VSA specialists may be less likely to discover the work there organically

### 4. Survey / mapping / review-oriented venues

Examples:

- [ACM Computing Surveys](https://dl.acm.org/journal/csur)

Why they fit:

- the manuscript contains a systematic mapping and literature transfer layer

Main risk:

- this is not a pure survey; it mixes review with original in-repository empirical evidence
- the current length and structure may not match a formal long-form survey expectation

### 5. Research software / reproducibility companion venues

Examples:

- [SoftwareX](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/softwarex)

Why they fit:

- strong repository, reproducibility, and artifact-management story

Main risk:

- the manuscript is primarily a scientific atlas and design framework, not just a software paper
- would likely need stronger software-engineering reframing

## Working recommendation

The most plausible near-term paths are:

1. a VSA / neural-computation venue if the manuscript is tightened around recoverability accounting and bounded empirical synthesis;
2. a broad methodology / reproducibility venue if the owner wants the atlas and evidence-discipline contribution foregrounded;
3. a survey-oriented venue only if the owner is willing to expand or reframe the systematic-mapping component substantially.
