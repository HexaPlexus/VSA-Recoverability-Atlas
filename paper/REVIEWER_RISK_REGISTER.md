# Reviewer Risk Register

| Likely criticism | Why it is plausible | Current defense | Missing evidence | Required wording | Claim to weaken |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| The scope is too broad for one paper. | The repository contains many hypotheses, baselines, and negative lines. | The paper is framed as a systematic mapping plus evidence atlas, not a single benchmark leaderboard. | A tighter visual summary of inclusion boundaries may still help. | Use 'mapping' and 'atlas' language consistently. | Any language implying complete field coverage. |
| The review is not systematic enough. | Repository-driven reviews often drift into narrative selection. | Frozen review protocol, search log, screening CSV, and typed comparability classes are added. | Could still add PRISMA-style counts later. | Describe this as a scoping/systematic mapping review, not a meta-analysis. | Any claim of exhaustive literature coverage. |
| Methods are not directly comparable. | Different algebras, task contracts, and hardware assumptions are mixed in the field. | Comparability classes and common-harness restrictions prevent unlawful numeric ranking. | More explicit table footnotes may still help. | Only DIRECT_COMMON_HARNESS rows are directly ranked. | Any cross-paper global frontier claim. |
| Too many results are development-only. | Many repository lines stopped at audited development evidence. | Claim ledger restricts scope and marks development-only claims explicitly. | More held-out confirmations would be needed for stronger empirical claims. | Use 'within the evaluated envelopes' and 'development-only' frequently. | Any universal positive or negative conclusion. |
| BCF contract favors BCF. | BCF is a native structured-code substrate with its own strengths. | The paper explicitly limits the conclusion to the clean common F=3 envelope and dual-view accounting. | Shared raw-noise contracts remain unmeasured. | Do not generalize beyond the tested clean common contract. | BCF is the best substrate overall. |
| Negative results lack generality. | Blocked lines may have only one or two envelopes. | The manuscript reframes them as bounded stop conditions, not impossibility theorems. | Broader reproduction across workloads would be needed for stronger closure. | Use 'not supported in the tested envelope' rather than 'disproved'. | Strong global negatives. |
| The hardware section is speculative. | No physical hardware is measured in this repository. | The section is explicitly labeled literature synthesis only. | Actual FPGA or in-memory measurements. | Hardware changes the frontier, not the accounting; not evaluated here. | Any measured hardware implication. |
| The repository is too large and heterogeneous. | Historical research accreted over multiple stages. | Evidence registry, hypothesis matrix, and failure atlas normalize the heterogeneity. | Optional pruning or archival packaging may still help for readers. | Describe the repository as an atlas, not a single polished system. | Any implication that every directory is equally central. |
| Single-author bias affects method selection and interpretation. | The repository reflects one research program. | Anti-NIH audits, adopted baselines, and explicit blocked verdicts are preserved rather than rewritten away. | Independent reproductions by others. | Acknowledge researcher degrees of freedom in threats to validity. | Any overly triumphant architectural interpretation. |
| Utility weights in the portfolio section are arbitrary. | Any scalar utility profile imposes scenario assumptions. | The manuscript now makes Pareto analysis primary and utility profiles illustrative only. | Domain-specific deployment workloads. | Utility profiles are scenario assumptions, not universal scores. | Any one-number method ranking. |
| There is no end-to-end workload. | Most repo tasks are synthetic but controlled contracts. | The paper positions the work as recoverability accounting under explicit contracts, not application-level benchmarking. | A domain workload with external validity. | Synthetic tasks are deliberate stress contracts for recoverability. | Direct application superiority. |
